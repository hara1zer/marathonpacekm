Tips for running Sub-4

  • Start controlled: avoid early surges and let the pace come to you in the first 5–10K.
  • Use checkpoints: focus on 10K, half, 30K, and 40K — not moment-to-moment GPS noise.
  • Fuel early: small, regular carbs from early in the race helps prevent the late collapse.
  • Don’t “bank time”: read why starting too fast costs minutes.

Sub-4 pace band FAQ

What pace is a Sub-4 marathon?

A Sub-4 marathon (3:59:59) averages about 5:41 per km (about 9:09 per mile).

What’s included on this pace band?

Key checkpoints: 5K, 10K, half, 30K, 40K and finish. You can print a wrist band or an A4 pacing sheet.

How do I print or save as PDF?

Click Wrist band (PDF) or A4 sheet (PDF). Your browser print dialog will open. Choose Save as PDF or print normally.
