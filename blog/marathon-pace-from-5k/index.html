Marathon pace from 5K time

You can estimate marathon pace from a 5K — but it’s one of the easiest ways to get an over-optimistic marathon goal. Why? A 5K reflects speed + VO₂max more than marathon-specific endurance and fueling durability.

Published: 2026-01-29 • Updated: 2026-01-29
If you only have a 5K time… Use the conservative range below, then validate with longer events when possible: 10K → marathon or Half → marathon.

Calculator (Riegel-style model)

A widely used prediction approach uses a power-law scaling of time vs distance (often called “Riegel” in running calculators). Shorter races generally require a more conservative endurance assumption when projecting to 42.195 km.

Riegel paper copies: RRTC PDF, alternate PDF.

5K → marathon calculator

If your longest runs aren’t close to 28–32 km and you haven’t practiced marathon fueling, choose 1.07–1.08.

Why 5K overpredicts marathon (common reasons)

  • Durability: the marathon exposes fatigue resistance and training depth.
  • Fueling: carbohydrate availability and intake strategy matter late in the race.
  • Pacing errors: small early overpacing can cause big late slowdowns.

Better alternatives (if you can get them)

  • Marathon pace from 10K time (stronger signal than 5K)
  • Marathon pace from half marathon time (usually best race predictor)
  • VDOT marathon pace (fitness concept + training intensities)
