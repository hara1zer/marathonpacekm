Marathon pace from half marathon time

If you have one race to predict a marathon, the half marathon is usually the best compromise: long enough to reflect endurance, short enough that fueling/pacing mistakes are less catastrophic than a full marathon.

How to pick the right range

  • Aggressive: you’ve built consistent volume, long runs, and tolerate goal pace late.
  • Balanced: decent base, but still building marathon-specific durability.
  • Conservative: first marathon, inconsistent long runs, uncertain fueling, or tough conditions.

Quick rule of thumb (for planning)

Many runners find the marathon is not “2× the half.” A small additional slowdown is normal as distance doubles, especially without marathon-specific training.
